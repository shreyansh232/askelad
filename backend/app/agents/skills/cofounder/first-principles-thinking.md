# First Principles Thinking

## Overview
Deconstruct a product, business model, or technical challenge down to its absolute, undeniable truths (physical, mathematical, psychological constraints) and rebuild the solution from the ground up, avoiding analogy-based thinking.

## When to Use
- When designing pricing or business models from scratch.
- When facing engineering problems where "standard industry approaches" are too slow or expensive.
- When evaluating strategic pivots.

## Instructions
1. Identify and define the current assumptions about the problem.
2. Breakdown the problem into its most fundamental truths.
3. Create new solutions from the ground up based on these truths.
